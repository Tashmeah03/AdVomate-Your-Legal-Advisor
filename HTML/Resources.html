Understand Your Legal Issues

Criminal Law

Criminal law deals with behavior that is considered harmful to society as a whole. It includes prosecution by the government of wrongful acts which are classified as crimes. Criminal law covers offenses such as murder, theft, assault, fraud, and other violations of public order.

Corporate Law

Corporate law governs the formation, operation, and dissolution of corporations. It covers matters such as corporate governance, mergers and acquisitions, securities regulation, and shareholder rights. This area of law ensures that businesses operate within legal frameworks.

Administrative Law

Administrative law governs the activities of administrative agencies of government. It includes the rules, regulations, and orders by which administrative agencies exercise their authority. This field covers regulatory compliance, government procedures, and public administration.

Constitutional Law

Constitutional law deals with the interpretation and implementation of the constitution. It covers fundamental rights, separation of powers, federalism, and the relationship between different branches of government. This field ensures that laws comply with constitutional principles.

Labour Law

Labour law governs the relationship between workers, employers, trade unions, and the government. It covers employment contracts, workplace safety, wages, working hours, discrimination, and collective bargaining. This field protects workers' rights and ensures fair employment practices.

Intellectual Property Law

Intellectual Property law protects creations of the mind, including inventions, literary and artistic works, designs, symbols, names, and images used in commerce. It covers patents, copyrights, trademarks, and trade secrets, ensuring creators can benefit from their innovations.

Family Law

Family law covers legal matters related to family relationships, including marriage, divorce, child custody, adoption, domestic violence, and property settlements. It aims to protect the rights and interests of family members.

Contract Law

Contract law governs the formation, performance, and enforcement of agreements between parties. It covers breach of contract, contract interpretation, and remedies for contractual disputes.

Property Law

Property law deals with the ownership and use of real and personal property. It covers property rights, land transactions, landlord-tenant relationships, and property disputes.

Tax Law

Tax law governs the collection of taxes and the resolution of tax disputes. It covers income tax, corporate tax, VAT, customs duties, and tax compliance matters.

Environmental Law

Environmental law focuses on protecting the environment and natural resources. It covers pollution control, environmental impact assessments, and sustainable development regulations.

Immigration Law

Immigration law governs the entry, stay, and status of foreign nationals. It covers visa applications, citizenship, deportation, and refugee protection.

Constitution of People's Republic of Bangladesh

The Constitution of Bangladesh is the supreme law of Bangladesh. It was adopted by the Constituent Assembly of Bangladesh on November 4, 1972, and came into effect on December 16, 1972. The Constitution establishes Bangladesh as a secular, democratic republic and enshrines fundamental human rights, the structure of government, and the rule of law. It has been amended seventeen times since its adoption, reflecting the evolving needs of the nation while maintaining its core democratic principles.
